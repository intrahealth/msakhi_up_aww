Who are we
We are Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation funded Manthan project (infomanthan@intrahealth.org) of IntraHealth International (www.intrahealth.org). We are testing a promising multi-media mobile phone based application, developed on android platform, called mSakhi, with ASHAs and ANMs in Uttar Pradesh, India. mSakhi aims to assist Accredited Social Health Activists (ASHAs)and Auxiliary Nurse Midwife (ANM) to perform their jobs more effectively by addressing some of the challenges faced by them in promoting and providing primary health services among the rural community.

Why mSakhi?
ASHAs are well-positioned in rural communities of India to improve maternal and newborn health outcomes. With more than 820,000 ASHAs trained, they are reaching the most impoverished and remote communities with key preventive health services.

What is mSakhi
mSakhi is an interactive mobile phone application for ASHAs and ANMs, it has three main features:

  • Self-learning and Counseling: audio, graphic images and short videos, serve as self-learning and counseling tool (http://youtu.be/YmEsRyYXnKQ)
  • Decision Support: case management including diagnosis, assessment, classification and categorization of HBNC treatment and referral (http://youtu.be/jIZvMHVebIE)
  • Real-Time Monitoring and Management: ANMs access to ASHA wise home visit schedule and completion status on a real time basis (http://youtu.be/jIZvMHVebIE)

How to use mSakhi

  • Register Pregnant Women:
    • Add the Name, LMP
    • along with PWs photo and record her name (as sound).
  • Counseling (audio-visual messages):
    • Select the month of pregnancy and show messages.
    • Select all and then select the message group and messages.
  • Report Birth
    • Select the PW
    • Add date of birth of the child and other related details.

p.s. ->Abortion and stillbirth can also be reported here

  • Postnatal home visits
    • Select the beneficiary and as per the calendar make the home visit.
    • Follow the IMNCI checklist and respond to the questions after examination and assessing the infant and mother.
    • Provide home based care to the beneficiary based on the IMNCI categorization. 
  • Report
    • Look at the Completed home visits and Death reports.
  • Report death
    • Report death of mother or newborn in the first 42 days on birth.
